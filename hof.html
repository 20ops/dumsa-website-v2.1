We had a sit-down with our distingushed Alumini chairman.

Read the interview below.

What inspired you to take on the role of DUMSAA chairman, and how does this role reflect your vision for the alumni community?

It's all about vision. I want to foster a strong, supportive, and engaged alumni network. I envision a community that actively mentors students, provides career guidance, facilitates networking opportunities, and contributes financially and intellectually to the advancement of the medical school. I believe that by leveraging the collective wisdom, experience, and resources of our alumni, we can significantly enhance the educational experience for current students and strengthen the reputation of DELSU Medical School on a national and international level.

What do you believe is the most important way DUMSAA can support students and alumni?

I believe the most crucial way DUMSAA can support students and alumni is through mentorship. By connecting current students with successful alumni in various medical fields, we can provide invaluable guidance, insights, and support throughout their academic and professional journeys. Mentorship can help students navigate the challenges of medical school, explore different career paths, and develop essential professional skills.

Many of us wonder what life after medical school is like. What advice would you give for making that transition smoother?

The transition to life after medical school can be both exciting and daunting. My advice is to embrace lifelong learning. The field of medicine is constantly evolving. Continuously update your knowledge and skills through postgraduate training, conferences, and professional development courses. It also helpd to build a strong professional network. Cultivate relationships with colleagues, mentors, and other healthcare professionals. Networking can open doors to new opportunities and provide invaluable support. Maintain a healthy work-life balance. Prioritize your well-being by maintaining a healthy lifestyle, pursuing personal interests, and spending quality time with loved ones. Find a mentor or support system. Seek guidance and support from experienced professionals who can offer valuable advice and encouragement.

Are there any exciting DUMSAA projects students can participate in, and how do you think this involvement would benefit us?

Yes, there are several exciting DUMSAA projects that students can participate in. Students can become mentees in mentorship programs, gaining valuable insights and guidance from experienced alumni. They can also volunteer as mentors to younger students, developing crucial leadership and mentorship skills. Furthermore, students can actively participate in community health outreach programs organized by DUMSAA, gaining invaluable experience in public health and community engagement. Attending and participating in alumni networking events provides students with opportunities to expand their professional network and gain valuable career insights. Additionally, contributing to fundraising efforts that support student welfare, scholarships, and other initiatives that benefit the medical school is a meaningful way for students to give back. Participating in these projects will afford students the opportunity to gain valuable experience in developing essential skills such as communication, teamwork, leadership, and community engagement. These experiences also facilitate the expansion of their professional network by connecting them with successful alumni and fostering relationships that can significantly benefit their future careers. Moreover, these endeavors allow students to make a positive impact on the lives of others and contribute to the betterment of society, fostering a sense of civic responsibility and community engagement.

Thinking back to your time as a DELSU medical student, is there a moment or challenge that shaped you profoundly?

Writing and publishing my first novel, 'Every Time We Meet,' was a pivotal moment for me. It was a powerful affirmation of my identity as a writer, a passion that has always burned within me. I wasn't going to let the demanding schedule and intense focus of medical school extinguish that precious flame. Instead, I saw it as a unique challenge – an opportunity to hone my discipline, manage my time with meticulous precision, and even use my writing skills to enhance my medical training. For example, crafting compelling narratives for my novel helped me develop strong communication skills, essential for effectively conveying complex medical information to patients. The meticulous research involved in building the world of 'Every Time We Meet' fostered a deep appreciation for detail and accuracy, invaluable traits for a physician. Looking back, I have absolutely no regrets. Publishing my novel has not only enriched my life as an artist but has also deepened my understanding of myself and my place in the world. It has proven that pursuing one's passions, even amidst the most demanding circumstances, is not only possible but can also lead to unexpected and profound rewards.

Outside of your work and alumni responsibilities, what hobbies or activities do you enjoy?

I write and write and write. I'm always writing – stories, poems, anything that comes to mind. Writing is a constant in my life. In fact, I LIVE TO WRITE. Writing is an integral part of who I am. It's a constant in my life – a flowing stream of ideas that I pour onto the page. Whether it's a short story, a poem, or simply jotting down my thoughts in a journal, I find myself constantly engaged in the act of creation. But writing isn't the only passion that fuels me. I'm deeply involved in sports, particularly football, which holds a special place in my heart. The thrill of the game, the camaraderie of the team, the sheer joy of competition – it all resonates deeply with me. I believe that these two seemingly disparate passions – the solitary pursuit of writing and the collective energy of team sports – both contribute to my personal growth. They teach me discipline, perseverance, the importance of both individual expression and collaborative effort, and the enduring satisfaction that comes from pushing my limits.

Finally, if you could share one message with all DELSU medical students, what would it be?

My message to all DELSU medical students is to never give up on your dreams. The path to becoming a doctor is challenging, but it is also incredibly rewarding. Embrace the challenges, learn from your mistakes, and never lose sight of your passion for medicine. Remember that you are part of a strong and supportive community, and we are here to help you succeed.
